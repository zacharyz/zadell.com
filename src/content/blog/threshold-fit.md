## What threshold means

[Set up the conceptual frame in ~150 words. The reader should leave this section knowing what the title means.]

- The lactate threshold — the line between effort that builds you and effort that breaks you down.
- The metaphorical threshold — between phases of life, between who you've been and who you're becoming.
- Name them both. Name that the app sits on the intersection.

## The diagnosis

[~300 words. What existing fitness apps actually optimize for, and why it isn't *you*.]

- Engagement, not progress. Streaks, kudos, leaderboards — dopamine architecture borrowed from social media.
- Generic plans dressed up as personalization.
- AI added as a chatbot, not a coach.
- The serious self-coached athlete and the casual user are treated identically because the metric is daily active users.
- Be specific. Name patterns precisely enough that a reader recognizes them. Optional: name products, if you're willing.

## What I keep wanting and not finding

[~250 words. The list. This is where the essay earns its frustration.]

- A coach with long memory of my training, an opinion about my goals, the willingness to say *back off this week.*
- Programming that adapts to actual recovery, not nominal calendar weeks.
- Data that's mine. Exportable. Not a feature of someone else's growth model.
- Quiet. No streaks. No congratulations for showing up.

## Why I'm the one to build it

[~200 words. Credential paragraph, but resist the resume voice. Make it feel like inheritance, not pitch.]

- 20+ years engineering at scale.
- Multiple marathons. Years of self-coached training.
- Mid-life: the rare intersection of "knows software deeply" + "knows the sport from inside" + "has the patience to build it slowly."

## What threshold.fit will be

[~250 words. The product description, told in plain language. No feature checklist — name the principles.]

- A coach you trust, built around what real coaches do (volume/intensity periodization, recovery primary, conservative progression).
- LLMs as the coaching surface — not a chatbot, a coach with memory and opinions.
- Honest data. No gamification. No streaks.
- Built for decades of training, not the next 90-day challenge.
- Your data, exportable. No walled garden.

## The personal stake

[~150 words. Why this matters to you specifically. Connect back to the threshold concept — building the threshold you want to live at.]

- Middle age + endurance + the goal of training meaningfully for the next 30 years.
- The tools available won't get you there.
- Building the tool you wish existed is the most direct expression of what you actually believe.

## Close

[~100 words. Invitation. What's coming. How to follow along.]

- Don't over-promise the timeline.
- Land back on the threshold metaphor: *you build the threshold you want to live at* (or your own version of that line).
